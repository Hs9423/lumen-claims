Know Your Enemy

How Agents Defraud Policyholders

Insurance agents exploit trust, complexity, and policy jargon to steal from ordinary citizens. Understanding how they operate is your first line of defense.

⚠ Warning Signs You've Been Defrauded
  • → Premium is much higher than quoted
  • → Policy documents never arrived
  • → Agent is unreachable after purchase
  • → Claim gets rejected without valid reason
  • → Policy features don't match promises
  • → Asked to pay in cash with no receipt
🎭

Misrepresentation of Policy

Agents falsely describe policy benefits, coverage, maturity amount, or terms to induce purchase. Victims discover the truth only at claim time — often years later.

Extremely Common
💸

Premium Misappropriation

Agents collect premium payments from policyholders but never deposit them with the insurance company — pocketing the money while the policy lapses unbeknownst to the victim.

High Severity
📋

Unauthorized Policy Changes

Agents forge signatures to alter beneficiaries, coverage levels, or policy terms without the policyholder's consent — often to earn higher commissions.

Criminal Offense
🔄

Churning / Twisting

Agents convince policyholders to surrender existing profitable policies and buy new ones — earning fresh commissions at the policyholder's financial loss due to surrender penalties.

IRDAI Violation
👻

Ghost Policy Fraud

Agents issue fake policy documents for policies that don't actually exist. Policyholders pay premiums for years, only to discover no policy was ever issued in their name.

Criminal Fraud
📑

False KYC & Form Manipulation

Agents fill application forms with incorrect details — understating age, hiding medical conditions, falsifying income — that later become grounds to reject legitimate claims.

Claim Denial Risk
🏦

Bancassurance Mis-selling

Bank employees push insurance products disguised as savings schemes, FDs, or investment products. Victims often don't know they bought insurance until they can't withdraw their money.

Most Reported
⏰

Free-Look Period Suppression

Agents hide or discourage use of the mandatory 15–30 day free-look period during which policyholders can cancel for a full refund. IRDAI mandates this right — agents who suppress it break the law.

Regulatory Breach
🎁

Inducement / Rebating

Agents offer unlawful gifts, cash-backs, or rebates to sell policies — a practice strictly prohibited under IRDAI regulations as it distorts the sale and hides policy shortcomings.

IRDAI Prohibition
Know Your Coverage

Types of Insurance in India

Every insurance product works differently — and frauds are designed to exploit the complexity of each. Understanding your insurance type is the first step to protecting yourself.

Life Insurance

Life insurance provides financial protection to your family upon your death or disability. India has the world's second-largest life insurance market, making it the most frequent fraud target. Regulated by IRDAI under the Insurance Act, 1938.

Types of Life Insurance Policies

  • Term Insurance: Pure protection, highest cover at lowest premium. No maturity benefit if insured survives.
  • Whole Life Insurance: Coverage for entire life (up to 100 years). Builds cash value.
  • Endowment Plans: Combines insurance + savings. Pays on death or maturity, whichever earlier.
  • Money-Back Plans: Periodic payouts during policy term + maturity/death benefit.
  • Child Plans: Secures child's education/marriage goals with waiver of premium on parent's death.
  • Pension/Annuity Plans: Retirement income solutions — regular payouts post vesting age.

⚠ Common Life Insurance Frauds

  • Promising guaranteed returns (SEBI-regulated, not insurer's liability)
  • Selling endowment as "tax-free savings scheme"
  • Withholding rider exclusions (suicidal death, war clause)
  • Churning profitable endowments for fresh commissions
  • Adding hidden riders without consent
  • False age/health declarations to lower initial premium
  • Premium collection without policy issuance

Health Insurance

Health insurance covers medical expenses from illness, injury, or hospitalization. Post-COVID demand has surged, creating fertile ground for both agent fraud and insurer claim rejections. Regulated by IRDAI; standardized products governed under the Standardisation of Health Insurance Products Circular.

Types of Health Insurance

  • Individual Health Plans: Coverage for a single person's hospitalization & treatment.
  • Family Floater Plans: One shared sum insured for entire family — risky if one member has large claims.
  • Critical Illness Plans: Lump-sum payment on diagnosis of listed critical diseases.
  • Senior Citizen Plans: Designed for those above 60; often with co-payment clauses.
  • Super Top-Up Plans: Kicks in after base coverage is exhausted — cost-effective addon.
  • Group/Corporate Health: Employer-sponsored; may have portability issues on resignation.

⚠ Common Health Insurance Frauds

  • Hiding sub-limits, room rent caps, co-payment clauses
  • Selling group policy as individual plan (no individual rights)
  • Not disclosing waiting periods for pre-existing diseases
  • Insurer denying cashless at last minute citing "policy exclusions"
  • False pre-existing disease mis-declaration at agent's instruction
  • Renewal loaded arbitrarily after claims without notice

Motor Insurance

Mandatory under the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988. Third-party insurance is compulsory; comprehensive coverage is optional. One of the most claim-intensive lines of insurance in India.

Types of Motor Insurance

  • Third-Party Liability: Mandatory — covers injury, death, or property damage to others.
  • Own Damage (OD): Covers your vehicle against accident, theft, natural calamities.
  • Comprehensive: Third-party + Own Damage in one policy.
  • Zero Depreciation Addon: Full part replacement cost without depreciation deduction.
  • Engine Protection Addon: Covers engine damage from waterlogging.
  • Pay As You Drive (PAYD): Usage-based, telematics-linked new product.

⚠ Common Motor Insurance Frauds

  • Downgrading policy (comprehensive to third-party) without informing customer
  • IDV (Insured Declared Value) set artificially low
  • Claim rejected citing policy gap the agent caused
  • Addon riders sold but not endorsed in policy
  • Insurer arbitrarily reducing claim settlement amount
  • Fraudulent surveyor assessments (insurer-linked surveyors)

Property & Home Insurance

Covers your home structure and/or contents against fire, flood, theft, earthquake, and other perils. Surprisingly under-penetrated in India yet highly exploitable by agents given the complexity of coverage definitions.

Types of Property Insurance

  • Building/Structure Insurance: Covers physical structure of the property.
  • Contents Insurance: Covers movable assets inside the home.
  • Fire & Allied Perils Policy: Classic coverage for fire, lightning, flood, earthquake.
  • Homeowners Comprehensive: Bundle of building + contents + liabilities.
  • Commercial Property: Covers business premises and assets.
  • Shop Package Policy: All-in-one for small business owners.

⚠ Common Property Insurance Frauds

  • Under-insuring property to reduce premium (leaves large claim gap)
  • Mis-describing property type/use (residential vs commercial)
  • Claim rejection citing undisclosed pre-existing damage
  • Insurer's surveyor undervaluing loss significantly
  • Not disclosing exclusions for specific perils like flood

Travel Insurance

Covers risks during domestic or international travel: medical emergencies, trip cancellation, baggage loss, flight delays. Most mis-sold alongside ticket bookings without explaining coverage.

Types of Travel Insurance

  • International Travel Insurance: Medical + trip cancellation + baggage — Schengen visa requirement.
  • Domestic Travel: Covers flight delays, baggage, accidental injury within India.
  • Student Travel Insurance: Medical + education interruption cover for abroad study.
  • Annual Multi-Trip: One policy for multiple trips in a year — best for frequent travelers.
  • Senior Citizen Travel: Higher medical sum insured, pre-existing disease cover.

⚠ Common Travel Insurance Frauds

  • Selling bare-minimum medical cover while claiming "full coverage"
  • Hiding adventure sports exclusions
  • Not disclosing pre-existing disease exclusions
  • Claim rejection for COVID-related trip cancellation with hidden exclusions
  • Insurer using overseas TPA delays to frustrate medical claims

ULIP & Investment-Linked Insurance

Unit Linked Insurance Plans (ULIPs) combine life insurance with market-linked investments. One of the most widely mis-sold products in India — often pushed as "mutual funds with insurance" while hiding lock-in periods and high charges.

Types of Investment Insurance

  • Standard ULIP: Premium split between insurance and market-linked fund units.
  • Guaranteed Return Plans: Non-linked, fixed returns — often poorly explained rate of return.
  • ULIP Child Plans: Long-term wealth creation for children's goals.
  • Retirement ULIPs: Market-linked pension accumulation products.
  • Whole Life ULIP: Long-duration market-linked cover with partial withdrawals.

⚠ Common ULIP / Investment Fraud

  • Comparing projected ULIP returns with FD rates dishonestly
  • Hiding 5-year lock-in period and heavy surrender charges
  • Not disclosing fund management charges (FMC), policy admin charges
  • Selling as "risk-free FD" when underlying funds are equity-linked
  • Switching funds without customer consent to earn trail commissions
  • Promising guaranteed NAV (not legally permissible)

Insurance Regulations & Your Rights

India has comprehensive insurance regulations. These are your weapons. Lumen Claims ensures every one of your rights is exercised to its fullest extent.

01
⚖️

IRDAI — Your Primary Regulator

The Insurance Regulatory and Development Authority of India (IRDAI) regulates all insurance companies and agents in India. It mandates transparency, fair claim settlement, and consumer protection. Policyholders can lodge complaints directly at IRDAI's Bima Bharosa portal. IRDAI's Circular on Protection of Policyholders' Interests requires insurers to settle death claims within 30 days and health claims within 30 days of receiving all documents.

IRDAI Act, 1999
02
🏛️

Insurance Ombudsman — Free Dispute Resolution

India has 17 Insurance Ombudsmen across the country, providing free and binding dispute resolution for claims up to ₹50 lakh. Complaints must be filed within 1 year of insurer's rejection. The Ombudsman can award compensation up to ₹50 lakh plus legal costs. The entire process is free for the policyholder — a right Lumen Claims leverages aggressively on your behalf.

Insurance Ombudsman Rules, 2017
03
📜

Free-Look Period — Your Right to Exit

IRDAI mandates a 15-day free-look period (30 days for distance marketing). If you're unsatisfied with your policy, you can return it and receive a full refund minus risk premium and medical examination costs. This right cannot be waived or overridden by any agent. If your agent hid this right, it constitutes a regulatory violation and entitles you to compensation.

IRDAI PPH Regulations, 2017
04
📱

IGMS — Online Grievance Portal

IRDAI's Integrated Grievance Management System (IGMS) allows policyholders to register complaints against insurance companies online. Insurers are mandated to resolve complaints within 15 days. If unresolved, IRDAI escalates. Lumen Claims files and tracks all IGMS complaints on your behalf, ensuring proper documentation and timely follow-up.

IRDAI (IGMS) Guidelines
05
⏱️

Claim Settlement Timelines

IRDAI has mandated strict claim settlement deadlines: Death claims must be settled within 30 days of documentation submission. If investigation is needed, a maximum of 90 days. Health claims — cashless within 1 hour, reimbursement within 30 days. Motor OD claims — within 7 days. Violation of these timelines entitles you to 2% monthly interest on delayed claim amounts.

IRDAI Claim Circular 2015
06
🚫

Agent Code of Conduct — Banned Practices

IRDAI regulations strictly prohibit agents from inducing policyholders with gifts or rebates, misrepresenting policy terms, withholding material information, collecting premiums in personal accounts, advising policy surrender for fresh commission, and soliciting without a valid IRDAI license. Violations invite license cancellation and criminal prosecution under IPC Section 420 (cheating).

IRDAI Agents Regulations, 2016
07
🏦

Policyholder Protection Fund

IRDAI's Policyholder Protection Fund (PPF) safeguards policyholders in case of insurer insolvency or non-payment. For life insurance, claims up to ₹5 lakh are protected. For non-life, interim payments can be made. Lumen Claims helps victims access this fund when standard claim routes fail, particularly in cases of insurer insolvency.

Insurance Act Section 38A
08
⚡

Consumer Courts — Deficiency in Service

Insurance fraud and wrongful claim rejection constitute "deficiency in service" under the Consumer Protection Act, 2019. Victims can file complaints in District (up to ₹1 Cr), State (₹1–10 Cr), or National (above ₹10 Cr) Consumer Commissions — and claim compensation for mental agony, legal expenses, and punitive damages in addition to the claim amount. Lumen Claims has a dedicated consumer court team.

Consumer Protection Act, 2019

Common Questions

Frequently Asked Questions

Everything you need to know before reaching out. If your question isn't here — just contact us.

Is there a time limit to file a complaint against insurance fraud?

+
Yes. For Insurance Ombudsman complaints, you must file within 1 year of the insurer's final rejection or non-response. Consumer Court complaints must be filed within 2 years of the fraud. IRDAI complaints have no strict time limit but should be filed promptly. Contact us immediately — delays can jeopardize your case.

My agent collected cash — is there any hope of recovery?

+
Yes, even in cash payment cases there are recovery routes. If the agent is licensed by IRDAI, the insurer has vicarious liability for licensed agent misconduct. We'll investigate policy issuance records, trace the agent through IRDAI's register, and pursue FIR-based criminal proceedings alongside civil recovery.

The insurance company says my claim is excluded — can you still help?

+
Absolutely. Many "exclusion-based" rejections are wrongful. If the exclusion was not disclosed at the time of sale, or if the agent concealed it, it may not be legally enforceable. We analyze every rejection and challenge it on both factual and legal grounds — many exclusion-based rejections can be successfully challenged on factual or legal grounds.

What is your fee structure?

+
We work on a pure contingency basis — you pay nothing upfront. Our fee is a percentage of the amount recovered, agreed upon before we start. If we don't recover money for you, you owe us nothing. Consultation, case assessment, and all initial filings are free of charge.

The agent is no longer reachable. Can I still file a complaint?

+
Yes. The primary liability lies with the insurance company for the actions of its licensed agents. Agent disappearance doesn't absolve the insurer. We file complaints directly against the insurance company via IRDAI and Ombudsman, and separately trace and file criminal complaints against the agent through appropriate channels.

My bank sold me an insurance policy I never wanted. Can I get a refund?

+
This is bancassurance mis-selling — one of the most reported frauds. If the bank employee failed to obtain your free and informed consent, misrepresented the product as an FD or savings scheme, or charged your account without explicit authorization, you have strong grounds for a full refund plus compensation under consumer protection law.

How long does the resolution process typically take?

+
It depends on the route. Insurer direct resolution: 15–30 days. IRDAI IGMS grievance: 30–45 days. Insurance Ombudsman: 60–90 days. Consumer Court: 3–18 months. We pursue multiple routes simultaneously to maximize speed. The Ombudsman typically concludes proceedings within 3 months.

Can I file a police FIR against a fraudulent agent?

+
Yes. Insurance fraud involving deliberate misrepresentation or premium misappropriation constitutes cheating under IPC Section 420, criminal breach of trust under Section 406, and forgery under Section 468 (if documents were forged). Lumen Claims assists with FIR preparation, complaint to Economic Offences Wing, and parallel civil recovery — the criminal route often accelerates civil settlement.
